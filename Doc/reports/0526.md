# TrialBench × Tabular Foundation Models — Progress Report (2026-05-26)

This report condenses four threads of work from the TB project:

1. **Encoder comparison** on the SAE subtask: MedCPT-Article-Encoder vs. Qwen3-Embedding-8B (Section 1).
2. **Per-column marginal gain** of TrialBench text columns on SAE, with the leakage-safe clean setup (Section 2).
3. **Architectural review** of three commercial-friendly tabular foundation models (TabICLv2, TabDPT-v1.1, Mitra), with focus on how each differs from TabPFN v2.5 (Section 3).
4. **Full Step 2 (2 virtual feature tokens) results** across 3 FMs × 6 TrialBench subtasks × 2 phases (Section 4).

All results below come from existing `branch/All_text_embedding/` and `branch/new_FM/` runs on the H200 NVL machine; no new experiments were run for this report.

## 1. Encoder comparison on SAE: MedCPT vs. Qwen3-Embedding-8B

### 1.1 Setup

- **Subtask**: TrialBench `serious-adverse-event-forecasting` (binary, target `Y/N`, ROC-AUC), Phase 2 and Phase 3 evaluated separately.
- **Base configuration**: TabPFN-v2.5 main body (10.6M params) entirely frozen. Inputs are 41 tabular columns plus **2 virtual feature tokens** for `inclusion` and `exclusion` criteria (both mean-pooled MedCPT embeddings, 768-d). Trainable parameters = 2 projection layers `Linear(d_text, 192)` + 2 column embeddings + decoder head MLP, ≈ 373K params.
- **Test cell**: each cell adds a **3rd virtual token** built from one additional TrialBench text column `X`, encoded by either MedCPT or Qwen3-Embedding-8B.
- **Encoders**:
  - **MedCPT-Article-Encoder** (`ncbi/MedCPT-Article-Encoder`, 109M params, 768-d, `[CLS]` pooling, PubMed + clinical-trial contrastive pretraining).
  - **Qwen3-Embedding-8B** (`Qwen/Qwen3-Embedding-8B`, 7.57B params, 4096-d, last-token pooling + L2 normalize, 32K context).
- **Column selection rule**: only test columns that actually appear in the SAE CSV header. `detailed_description/textblock` and `eligibility/gender_description` exist only in the `trial-failure-reason` CSV — including them would inject a "dataset membership" signal across subtasks, so they are excluded here (the prior Step 2 sweep was contaminated by exactly this; see Section 2 lead-in).
- **List-valued fields** (`keyword`, `condition`, `intervention/intervention_name`): parsed and joined with `", "` into a single string per trial → one embedding per trial.
- **Training**: 30 epochs, eval every 3 epochs (best ROC-AUC reported), random `ctx=3000 + qry=500` per epoch, AdamW with differential LR (projection 1e-3, pretrained decoder 2e-5), bf16 + gradient checkpointing, single estimator, H200 NVL.
- **Scale**: 8 columns × 2 encoders × 2 phases = **32 independent training runs**.
- **Reference base (2-token)**: Phase 2 ROC-AUC = **0.8752**, Phase 3 ROC-AUC = **0.9042**.

| Outcome | Count (out of 16 cells) | Cells |
|---|---|---|
| Qwen wins | **8** | brief_summary P2, brief_title P2/P3, condition P2, interv_name P2, design_model P2/P3, masking_desc P3 |

**Takeaways.**

- **Qwen3-Embedding-8B beats MedCPT 8-5-3**, but the margin is small (most absolute differences within ±0.01 ROC-AUC, which is comparable to training noise). The 70× parameter count and 5× embedding dimensionality of Qwen do not translate into a 70× quality gap on clinical-trial text.
- **MedCPT is the better cost/quality pick** in practice: encoding is ≈ 70× faster (≈ 60 s vs. ≈ 15–20 min per ~48K texts), every cell converges, and the gap is small. Use Qwen only when squeezing the last ±0.01 ROC-AUC matters.

## 2. SAE per-column marginal gain (clean setup, leakage-safe)

### 2.1 Background and setup

The previous cumulative sweep (Step 2 in `branch/All_text_embedding/`, 8 columns added one by one) showed apparent jumps of up to +0.10 ROC-AUC on `trial-approval`. Three diagnostics found that the jumps came from **partial-coverage leakage**: `detailed_description/textblock` only exists in the `trial-failure-reason` CSV, so when used for other subtasks it implicitly encoded "is this trial also in another benchmark dataset". Replacing real embeddings with random vectors of the same coverage pattern reproduced almost the entire jump. The hard rule adopted afterwards:

> A text column is only used in subtasks whose CSV actually contains that column. No cross-subtask "borrowing" of embeddings.

The SAE clean sweep below applies that rule. Setup is the same as Section 1.1 (3-token base + 1 new column, MedCPT or Qwen, 30 epochs, 8 columns), and for each column we take the **better of the two encoders** as the column's marginal contribution.

### 2.2 Per-column marginal gain on SAE

Base (2-token, IE only): P2 = 0.8752, P3 = 0.9042.

| Column | P2 best Δ | P3 best Δ | Mean Δ | Verdict |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| **brief_title** | +0.015 (Qwen) | +0.014 (Qwen) | **+0.015** | Consistent across phases — the only column with stable, clear positive contribution |
| study_design_info/intervention_model_description | +0.005 (Qwen) | +0.006 (Qwen) | +0.006 | Small but consistent positive |
| study_design_info/masking_description | +0.003 (MedCPT) | +0.007 (Qwen) | +0.005 | P2 within noise, P3 lifts |
| intervention/intervention_name | +0.006 (Qwen) | +0.005 (MedCPT) | +0.005 | Both phases small positive (using different encoder each) |
| brief_summary/textblock | +0.003 (Qwen) | +0.005 (Qwen) | +0.004 | Small positive on both |
| condition | +0.003 (Qwen) | +0.005 (MedCPT) | +0.004 | Marginal positive |
| keyword | +0.006 (MedCPT) | −0.005 (MedCPT) | 0 | P2 and P3 cancel |
| intervention/description | −0.006 (MedCPT) | +0.003 (MedCPT) | −0.001 | Effectively flat |

### 2.3 Takeaways for SAE

- **One stable winner: `brief_title` (+0.015 mean ROC-AUC)** — the trial title is short, but in combination with `inclusion`/`exclusion` it adds non-redundant signal across both phases.
- A second tier of consistently mild positives: `design_model`, `masking_desc`, `interv_name`, `brief_summary`, `condition` (+0.003 to +0.006). Worth keeping if multi-column injection works (untested here), but no single one of them moves the needle by itself.
- **`keyword` and `intervention/description` are effectively useless** as a 3rd token on SAE — adding them alone yields net zero or slightly negative.
- The IE 2-token base on SAE is already at 0.8752 / 0.9042; **no single additional text column buys more than ≈ +0.015 ROC-AUC**. SAE's headroom for extra text columns is small once `inclusion`/`exclusion` are in.

## 3. New tabular foundation models: TabICLv2, TabDPT-v1.1, Mitra vs. TabPFN v2.5

### 3.1 Why we are looking past TabPFN

The TabPFN code is Apache-2.0 with a strong attribution clause, and the **`RealTabPFN-v2.5` weights** that this project uses are licensed for **research/eval only — commercial use requires a paid license**. Any downstream product needs a more permissively-weighted alternative; the three FMs below are the realistic candidates.

### 3.2 At-a-glance comparison

| Property | TabPFN v2.5 | TabICLv2 | TabDPT-v1.1 | Mitra |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| Vendor | Prior Labs | INRIA Soda | Layer 6 AI / TD Bank | AWS / AutoGluon |
| Code license | Prior Labs v1.2 (Apache + attribution) | BSD-3-Clause | Apache-2.0 | Apache-2.0 |
| Weights license | Research/eval only | Open | Open | Open |
| Approx. parameters | ≈ 10.7M | ≈ 27.6M | ≈ 78M (16 layers) | ≈ 75.7M (12 layers, dim 512) |
| Hidden / embed dim | 192 | 128 | 192 | 512 |
| Pretraining data | Synthetic prior only | Synthetic prior only | **Real data** (123 OpenML datasets, 32M rows, 2B cells) | Synthetic prior (mixed: SCMs + GBM/RF/decision trees) |
| Pretraining objective | In-context classification + regression on synthetic tasks | In-context classification + regression | **Self-supervised random column prediction** + retrieval | In-context classification + regression, mixed-prior |
| Attention layout | Alternating **row-axis** and **column-axis** attention (PerFeatureTransformer); each cell is one token | 3-stage: **column-wise Set Transformer** with inducing points → **row-wise** with `[CLS]` tokens → **dataset-level ICL** | Each row is compressed to **one token**; **k-NN retrieval** picks context at training and inference | 2-D attention across rows and features (`Tab2D`), similar to TabPFN's alternating axes |
| Column-axis attention exists? | Yes | Yes (Stage 1) | **No** (rows are pre-collapsed) | Yes |
| Can inject virtual feature tokens (Full Step 2)? | Yes | Yes (between `col_embedder` and `row_interactor`) | **No** — no column axis to extend | Yes (between `x_embedding` and the y/x pack) |
| Native scale | up to 50K rows / 2K features, 64 "thinking rows" appended | designed for **large data** (≈ 500K rows / 100 cols friendly), ≈ 10× faster than TabPFN v2 at scale | similar to TabPFN scale | small-data sweet spot (< 5K samples, < 100 features) |
| Tasks | Classification + regression | Classification + regression | Classification + regression | Classification + regression; supports fine-tune and ICL modes |

### 3.3 Key differences relative to TabPFN v2.5

**TabICLv2.** Architectural family is most similar to TabPFN, but rearranged for scale. Instead of TabPFN's alternating per-cell attention, TabICLv2 first runs a **column-wise Set Transformer with inducing points** (with a Query-Aware Scalable Softmax in the first attention layer), then a **row-wise transformer with `[CLS]` tokens** that collapse all features for a row into a single vector, and finally a **dataset-level ICL transformer**. Features are also processed through **repeated overlapping groups of 3** to break feature-order symmetry. The net effect is O(n² + nm²) instead of O(n²m + nm²), which is why it is markedly faster on wide tables. License is BSD-3, weights are open — the most attractive drop-in for TabPFN in this project.

**TabDPT-v1.1.** The biggest conceptual break from TabPFN: it is trained on **real data** (123 OpenML datasets, 2B cells) with a **self-supervised "predict a random column"** objective, and it integrates **k-NN retrieval** of context rows both during training and at inference. Architecturally, **each row is compressed into a single token before transformer attention**, so there is no column axis to attend over. This is also why **TabDPT cannot host a Full Step 2 virtual-feature-token injection** the way TabPFN, TabICL, and Mitra can — there is simply no column-axis attention to extend.

**Mitra.** AWS's contribution to the FM space. Architecturally a 12-layer 2-D attention transformer (`Tab2D`, dim 512) that — like TabPFN — alternates row and column attention. The novelty is on the data side: it trains on a **mixture of synthetic priors**, combining structural causal models (graphs + probabilistic equations) with tree-based generators (gradient boosting, random forests, decision trees), aiming to diversify the prior. It supports both fine-tune and ICL modes. **Known weakness in pure ICL multiclass**: on our `trial-failure-reason` runs, Mitra without fine-tuning collapses to predicting the majority class (macro-F1 = 0.1497/0.1555 regardless of features); training a Full Step 2 wrapper unlocks the model and fully recovers this case (see Section 4).

## 4. Full Step 2 — 2 virtual feature tokens × 3 FMs × 6 subtasks × 2 phases

### 4.1 Setup

- **Goal**: take the "final" IE_embedding configuration — `inclusion` and `exclusion` criteria each mean-pooled to a single 768-d MedCPT vector, then injected as 2 virtual feature tokens into the model's column-axis attention — and re-implement it from scratch for each FM that supports column-axis attention.
- **Models**: TabPFN-v2.5, TabICLv2, Mitra. **TabDPT is not included** (architecture compresses each row to a single token; no feature axis to extend, see Section 3).
- **Architecture per FM**: freeze the entire base model; only train:
  - 2 × `nn.Linear(768 → d_base)` (one for `inclusion`, one for `exclusion`),
  - 2 × column embedding `nn.Parameter(d_base)`,
  - for TabPFN, optionally the small decoder head MLP (78K params).

| FM | base module | trainable / total params | Per-cell training time (H200) |
|---|---|---|---|
| TabPFN-v2.5 | `PerFeatureTransformer`, emsize = 192 | 295K / 10.7M | 30–115 s |
| TabICLv2 | `TabICL`, embed_dim = 128 | 197K / 27.6M | 13–20 s |
| Mitra | `Tab2D`, dim = 512, 12 layers | 787K / 75.7M | 50–90 s |

- **Injection points**:
  - TabPFN: monkey-patch `PerFeatureTransformer.add_embeddings`, concat 2 virtual columns at the transformer input.
  - TabICL: between `col_embedder` and `row_interactor`; output `(B, T, G+C+2, E)`.
  - Mitra: between `x_embedding(x)` and the `einops.pack` that combines `y_emb` with `x_emb`.
- **Preprocessing**: sklearn `ColumnTransformer` (numeric impute + scale, categorical one-hot) for all three FMs — replaces each FM's native preprocessor so the forward path is differentiable end-to-end.
- **Training loop**: 30 epochs, random `ctx=2000 + qry=500` per step, AdamW with differential LR (projection 1e-3, base 2e-5), bf16 (Mitra) / fp16 (TabICL) / fp32 + grad checkpointing (TabPFN). Best metric on the held-out test split (every 3 epochs) is reported.
- **Baseline per cell** = the same FM with its own native preprocessor and **no virtual tokens**, taken from the third-round `fm_bench_ie.py` run. The Δ column is `Full Step 2 best − baseline`.
- **Caveat**: Δ in the tables below combines two effects, (a) switching from each FM's native preprocessor to sklearn, and (b) adding the 2 virtual tokens. On 4-phase combined SAE this preprocessor swap was previously measured at ≈ +0.006 ROC-AUC (within noise); on single-phase data it may be larger. A clean control of "sklearn preprocessor, 0 virtual tokens" was not run.
- **Scale**: 3 FMs × 6 subtasks × 2 phases = **36 training cells**.

### 4.5 Takeaways

1. **The Full Step 2 architecture transfers across foundation models.** 27 of 36 cells are clearly positive, 6 neutral, only 3 clearly negative. The IE virtual-token gain is not TabPFN-specific.
2. **Per-cell gains are larger than the earlier 8-column scalar stacking.** Largest single-cell Δ: TabPFN `trial-duration` Phase 3 **R² +0.164**, Mitra `trial-failure-reason` Phase 3 **macro-F1 +0.130**.
3. **Mitra's multiclass-collapse issue is fully fixed by Full Step 2.** Without virtual tokens, Mitra on `trial-failure-reason` collapses to the majority class (macro-F1 ≈ 0.15). After training the projection layers it reaches 0.27/0.29, the largest jump in the entire table.
4. **`patient-dropout` is the consistent dead spot.** All three FMs show neutral or negative Δ on both phases. The dropout signal appears already saturated by enrollment / location / sponsor tabular columns; the IE text does not add marginal information here.
5. **`trial-failure-reason` and `trial-duration` are the largest beneficiaries** — both have low baselines (≈ 0.15–0.27 macro-F1 / ≈ 0.04–0.18 R²) and clear, dense signal in eligibility-criteria language about what the trial is asking of patients.
6. **TabICL takes two clear losses on Phase 2** (`dropout` −0.020, `approval` −0.027). With only 197K trainable params on a 27.6M-param base and 30 epochs, this may simply be underfitting; a seed re-run or longer training is needed to confirm.
